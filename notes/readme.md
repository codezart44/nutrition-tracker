

## NOTES

USDA FDC, food databases

Foundation Foods:
    Foundation Foods represent basic, unbranded, and minimally processed food items.
    These foods are typically raw or whole ingredients that form the basis of many recipes and dishes.
    Foundation Foods provide detailed nutrient information for common ingredients like fruits, vegetables, meats, grains, etc.
    They are primarily used for calculating nutrient values in recipes, menu planning, and dietary analysis.
SR Legacy Foods:
    SR Legacy Foods refers to food items from the previous USDA database called the Standard Reference (SR).
    The Standard Reference was a widely used database before the introduction of FoodData Central.
    These legacy food items are included in FoodData Central for backward compatibility and reference purposes.
    The SR Legacy Foods category may contain a wide variety of food items, including both branded and unbranded products.
Survey Foods (FNDDS):   ⭐️  # Food and Nutrient Database for Dietary Studies
    Survey Foods, also known as the Food and Nutrient Database for Dietary Studies (FNDDS), are based on national surveys.
    FNDDS provides nutrient data for foods commonly consumed by the U.S. population.
    These foods represent a comprehensive collection of commercially available products consumed by individuals during the survey period.
    Survey Foods are particularly useful for analyzing dietary patterns, evaluating nutrient intakes, and conducting nutritional research.
Branded Foods:
    Branded Foods category includes food items that are commercially produced and have specific brand names.
    These foods are typically packaged products available in the market, such as snacks, beverages, frozen meals, and more.
    Branded Foods provide detailed nutrient information specific to the branded product.
    This category is useful for tracking nutrient values of specific branded products, comparing different brands, and analyzing the nutritional content of packaged foods.
Experimental Foods:
    The Experimental Foods category includes food items that are still being evaluated or are part of ongoing research studies.
    These foods may not have complete nutrient data or may be subject to changes and updates.
    Experimental Foods are not as widely used as the other categories and may have limited availability and documentation.
